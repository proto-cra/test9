Where to send international waiver and non-resident employer certification applications

Waiver application

You can submit all waiver and non-resident employer certification applications by mail or online using the Submit Documents feature on My Account for Individuals, Represent a Client, and My Business Account.

Individual Tax Number (ITN) applications should be submitted separately and in advance of your waiver and non-resident employer certification applications to avoid delays. To apply, follow the instructions found at Applying for an Individual Taxation Number. 

The charts below explain the options for submitting completed waiver applications, including those related to non-resident actors and behind the scenes personnel in the film and television industry, as well as non-resident employer certification applications (RC473).

If you are submitting supporting documents in response to a request for additional information, please include a cover page with the following information to ensure the documents are directed to the appropriate area:

  • The name of the waivers officer or person who requested the information;
  • The Centre of Expertise or tax services office where that person is located; and
  • If available, the case/file number provided or your tax identification number (social insurance number, individual tax number, business number).

Waiver applications - General (Regulation 102 and 105)

My Account
Submitting through My Account

Submit the completed form asking for a waiver and supporting information via My Account.

Note 

T1261 Individual Tax Number (ITN) applications may not be submitted through My Account. They must be submitted separately by following the instructions at Applying for an Individual Taxation Number or  follow the instructions on the back of the T1261 form.

  1. Log into My Account.
  2. Select the “Submit Documents” option located in the upper right corner of the screen.
  3. At the bottom of the screen, click on “without a case or reference number”.
  4. Select submission reason:
    • Select “Send documents related to Certificate of Compliance, International Waivers, Tax Shelter Identification Number or Clearance Certificate”.
  5. Click the “Next” button.
  6. Select submission reason:
    • Select “Send request for a waiver of withholding (Regulation 102 and 105, RC473) and supporting documents”.
  7. Click the “Next” button.
  8. Browse and navigate to the required file(s).
    • Multiple files totalling up to 500MB can be attached by pressing and holding the CTRL key while selecting your files. A 10 file limit per session also applies.
  9. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  10. Select “Attach file”.
  11. When all files have been attached, click “Submit file(s)”.
  12. Record the Confirmation number and Case/Reference number.

Submitting additional documents

  1. Log into My Account.
  2. Select “Submit Documents” option located in the upper right hand corner of the screen.
  3. Enter the Case/Reference number you received when you submitted your waiver or non-resident certification application through this portal. If you do not have this number, or you have not submitted documents through this portal previously, use the steps outlined above for a new submission.
  4. Browse and navigate to the required file(s).
  5. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  6. Select “Upload file”.
  7. When all files have been attached, click “Confirm”.
Represent A Client
Submitting through Represent A Client 

Submit the completed form asking for a waiver and supporting information via Represent a Client. 

Note 

T1261 Individual Tax Number (ITN) applications may not be submitted through Represent a Client. They must be submitted separately by following the instructions at Applying for an Individual Taxation Number or follow the instructions on the back of the T1261 form.

Submitting a new waiver or non-resident certification application
  1. Log into Represent a Client.
  2. Do not enter a SIN or BN.
  3. Select “Submit Documents” from the options on the left hand side of the screen.
  4. Under “Select one of the following”, select: "Representative related: Documents on behalf of yourself as a representative".
  5. Select “No” to the question “Do you have a case or reference number?”.
  6. Click the “Next” button.
  7. Select “Send request for a waiver of withholding (Regulation 102 and 105, RC473) and supporting documents”.
  8. Click the “Next” button.
  9. Browse and navigate to the required file(s).
    • Multiple files totalling up to 500MB can be attached by pressing and holding the CTRL key while selecting your files. A 10 file limit per session also applies.
  10. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  11. Select “Attach file”.
  12. When all files have been attached, click “Submit file(s)”.
  13. Record the Confirmation number and Case/Reference number.

Submitting additional documents

  1. Log into Represent a Client.
  2. Do not enter a SIN or BN.
  3. Select “Submit Documents” from the options on the left hand side of the screen.
  4. Under “Select one of the following”, select: "Representative related: Documents on behalf of yourself as a representative".
  5. Select “Yes” to the question “Do you have a case or reference number?”.
  6. Click the “Next” button.
  7. Enter the Case/Reference number you received when you submitted your waiver or non-resident certification application through this portal. If you do not have this number, or you have not submitted documents through this portal previously, use the steps outlined above for a new submission.
  8. Browse and navigate to the required file(s).
  9. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  10. Select “Attach file”.
  11. When all files have been attached, click “Submit file(s)”.
